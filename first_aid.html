First Aid Guides

Quick, life‑saving steps to follow before professional help arrives.

Severe bleeding
  • Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth for at least 5 minutes.
  • Elevate the injured area if possible; do not remove soaked cloth, add another layer.
  • If bleeding doesn’t stop, use a tourniquet above the wound and call emergency services.
Unconscious but breathing
  • Place in recovery position on their side, keep airway open.
  • Check breathing regularly; keep warm; do not give food or drink.
  • Call emergency services and monitor until help arrives.
Burns
  • Cool the burn under running water for 20 minutes.
  • Do not apply ice, butter, or toothpaste; cover loosely with clean cloth.
  • Seek medical help for large/deep burns or if face/hands/genitals are affected.
